Weapons and Armour

Now we're getting to the fun stuff. With five skill trees and five weapons for each one, there's a fair amount of customizability in terms of what you can go for at each stage of the game.

When you're looking at the tables, you will find that the first two tiers of each weapon type do nothing, so is there even a difference at all? You would know the answer if you read the previous section. Your weapon type influences the proc rate and/or damage of various passive skills in your skill trees. If you invest in a particular skill tree, choose a matching wand to maximize the effect of your skills. That being said, I'm not going to do a writeup on tier 1 or tier 2 weapons because there is nothing else interesting to cover. As I've stated before, you can check out neoquest.guide for detailed info on proc rates. I think you'd be surprised at how low some of the rates are.

Fire Weapons
Weapon Name Weapon Damage Special Skills
Red Wand 3 None
Gold Wand 6 None
Volcano Wand 10 Randomly cast Lava Flow to deal 20 Fire damage to the enemy
Fire Staff 15 Lets the user cast the Magma Burst ability, dealing 40 Fire damage to the enemy. Is recastable after a cooldown period
Firedrop Staff 20 Randomly cast Blast Furnace, dealing 30 Fire damage to the enemy for three turns (90 damage total) 5% (56/1102)

So you're interested in Fire weapons. I hate to say it, but you're probably going to be disappointed until about level 42. Fire itself is not a bad tree, I can't say the same for most of the weapons.

If I didn't know any better, I would have thought that the Volcano Wand was bugged with how little it procs. It may as well not have an ability at all, and its only purpose is to increase the damage of Fire Weapons when it procs.

Moving on to the Fire Staff, what a unique name. Anyway, you get a skill that lets you deal 40 damage on command. Sounds pretty good, right? Well, not exactly. At this point in the game, if you are investing in Fire, your basic attacks plus Fire Weapons skill procs are probably doing around that much damage anyway. Heck, you have Fireball that does 45 damage on a way lower cooldown. It's fine, but it's not great. Then again, you probably don't have much of a choice.

The Firedrop Staff is a completely different beast from any Fire weapon you have seen so far. Recall that the flaw of the Fire Staff was that it made you use a turn on an ability just to deal the same amount of damage as your basic attack. The Firedrop Staff solves this issue by making its skill a powerful passive burn in the form of Blast Furnace. The ability has no cooldown, and multiple procs stack the duration. This weapon makes random encounters a breeze and also deals respectable damage against bosses when it isn't resisted. Unlike other fire weapons, this one actually does the work for you.

Ice Weapons
Weapon Name Weapon Damage Special Skills
Blue Wand 3 None
Steel Wand 6 None
Glacier wand 10 Randomly cast Avalanche, dealing 20 ice damage to the enemy
Ice Staff 15 Lets the user cast Ice Shield: for the next 6 turns, successful basic attacks against you cause the attacker to take 10 ice damage
Iceheart Staff 20 Lets the user cast Ice Wind: For the next several rounds, randomly freeze (an actual stun unlike Heart of Ice) the enemy for one turn

So you want to use Ice weapons. The good news is that your endgame staff is among the best in the game. The bad news is that your midgame options are essentially useless, and I really do mean it when I say that.

Similar to how half of the Fire and Ice skill trees are carbon copies of each other, the Avalanche Wand may very well be a copy of the Volcano Wand. It's not very interesting, but it gets the job done to increase your Ice Weapons damage and enable Heart of Ice.

On first glance, maybe the Ice Staff sounds sort of interesting. It basically puts a damage shield on you, dealing backlash damage to the enemy. The only problem? 10 damage at this stage of the game is almost laughable, not to mention how the enemy needs to land a successful basic attack on you for it to even trigger. The only reason it's an upgrade over the Avalanche Wand is the higher weapon damage. Use this wand only for as long as you have to and push for the next tier.

The Iceheart Staff is possibly my favourite weapon in the game, and for very good reason. Not only does it fix Ice's flaw of not having a real stun, but Ice Wind consistently works on any opponent and with any playstyle. I don't believe that it can be resisted at all, so the question is only whether you get a proc or not. It doesn't matter if you want to spam basic attacks or all in with your ten points in Glacier Strike. You cast the skill and go back to what you were doing while it stuns the opponent over and over. The weapon is great for farming and bossfighting, and Ice Wind may very well be the best castable skill in the game. The Ice skill tree may fall off lategame, but this weapon completely makes up for it. Do yourself a favour an try it out even if you aren't running an Ice build.

Shock Weapons
Weapon Name Weapon Damage Special Skills
Yellow Wand 3 None
Bronze Wand 6 None
Storm Wand 10 Randomly cast Thunder Strike, stunning the enemy for one turn
Shock Staff 15 Lets the user cast Weakness, lowering the enemy's defense against basic attacks
Thunderstar Staff 20 Randomly casts Staggering Stun, stunning the enemy for two rounds 5% (95/1880)

I think everyone who knows anything about this game knows just how broken the Shock skill tree is, and Shock weapons do a good job at building on that. Despite the simplicity of the status effect, stun is the strongest status effect in the game, and these weapons generally increase your chance of inflicting it on your enemies.

Like I said above, Shock weapons are generally simple, and the Storm Wand is no exception. It amplifies your chance of proccing Shock Weapons, and it also has the chance to give you a free turn. What's not to like? The proc rate is just as bad as the other weapons in this tier, but we don't talk about that.

Moving on, the Shock Staff's active skill doesn't make much sense to me in terms of synergy. Lowering the enemy's physical defense so you can hit harder is useful, but the point of Shock is rendering the enemy useless while you wail on them. The extra damage should just be a bonus. It's not my favourite weapon, but it's perfectly functional.

The Thunderstar Staff is very simple and very good. I think you'll notice that there is a general trend here that the passive skills of final weapon upgrades are very powerful. Staggering Stun offers you up to two turns of stun for free while not taking up your turn to activate it. If you are running Shock, you will want it simply for the sake of proccing Shock Weapons more often. The staff is very powerful for both boss fights and random encounters, saving you from entire turns of damage, which could mean 100+ HP a turn in the endgame. Highly recommended for Shock builds.

Spectral Weapons
Weapon Name Weapon Damage Special Skills
Black Wand 3 None
Iron Wand 6 None
Spectral Wand 10 Randomly cast Absorbing Stone, absorbing a certain amount of an enemy's incoming basic attack
Spectral Staff 15 Lets the user cast Rage of Light, increasing your attack and defence for a certain number of turns. Is recastable after a cooldown period
Shadowgem Staff 20 Lets the user cast Elemental Resistance, increasing your resistance against all elemental blasts for a certain number of turns. Is recastable after a cooldown period

The Spectral weapon selection provides a surprisingly versatile set of abilities, with the endgame staff doing something a bit different from what you might expect. I say this because the entire Spectral tree is focused solely on negating and countering enemy basic attacks, and it is very powerful at doing so, but I will talk about it a bit later.

The Mountain Wand is an extremely forgettable weapon. Every once in a blue moon, its ability will negate a portion of the enemy's attack. Granted, at the level you get it, the damage negated isn't negligible, but the proc rate is abysmal like most other weapons in this tier. Even worse, the lizard enemies right after Gors on the way to Rollay focus especially on heavy elemental skill usage, further decreasing the weapon ability's utility. All that said, it still provides the bonus to Spectral Weapons' defence increase, potentially making it worth it after Rollay.

The Spectral Staff has the misfortune of being in would I could call a pretty average tier of weapons with the exception of one. It is stil an upgrade over the Mountain Wand, and you do get the castable Rage of Light, but its effects are hard noticeable in combat. Of course, if you are running a Spectral build, then you may have fun experimenting with this weapon for the boost to Spectral Weapons, but the healing from the Life Staff may still be more desirable.

Lastly, the Shadowgem Staff has a very interesting niche. This is the weapon that I mentioned that goes out of its way to provide something that its skill tree does not. If you couldn't tell from the name, Elemental Resistance raises your resistance to fire, ice, and stun attacks. I guess the intention here was to provide some defence against what the Spectral tree itself does not cover. I think it's an interesting idea and actually quite a strong ability against endgame bosses, but there are a few things to note about it. Firstly, it doesn't provide resistance against the 100 HP drains that are very common in the endgame (I don't think anything does), and the cooldown is a little long for the effect, not to mention how you have to spend a valuable turn casting a purely defensive skill that doesn't provide value against basic attacks. There is an argument to use this weapon with heavy Shock/Spectral builds to maximize your resistance against those deadly elemental attacks from endgame bosses, but I do prefer both of those trees' endgame weapons over the Shadowgem Staff. Nonetheless, this is still a genuinely good endgame weapon that grants the Spectral tree its full power at nullifying basic attacks, and I recommend at least giving it a try.

Life Weapons
Weapon Name Weapon Damage Special Skills
White Wand 3 None
Silver Wand 6 None
Nature Wand 10 Randomly cast Spirit of Oak, healing the user for 25 hp.
Life Staff 15 Lets the user cast Spirit of Growth, healing the user for 100 hp
Moonstone Staff 20 Heals the user for 4 hp every turn. In addition, randomly cast Spirit of Life, healing the user for 30 hp

Life weapons are arguably the most universally useful weapons in the game. You will always need more HP, and they do a good job of giving it to you. That's literally it. The best part about Life weapons is that the Life skill tree basically doesn't depend on the usual weapon + skill tree synergy to enhance its skills. You can use Life skills with other weapon types, and that means you can also use Life weapons with other skill trees quite effectively.

The Nature Wand is arguably broken for when you usually craft it. You are probably sitting right around level 22 (130 hp) when you beat Gors, so this thing is healing you almost 20% of your health bar when it procs, and the proc rate isn't completely abysmal like the other weapons in this tier either. For reference, the best Life weapon in the game heals you for 30 hp on skill proc. Maybe that all doesn't sound like much to you, but don't underestimate the number of potions this weapon will save you. If you are running a full Life build along with it (highly recommended), you will hardly have to touch your potions.

The Life Staff is a very famous weapon, and for good reason. 100 HP is a huge amount at any point in the game, and you get this thing around level 33 (185 hp). That's more than half your health. Pretty good, right? But it gets better. You might look at the massive 21 turn cooldown on this thing and think that it's a little high even for such a powerful skill, and I would agree. However, you have to remember that cooldowns don't carry over battles. That's right: you get a free 100 HP heal available for every battle. That makes the Life Staff beyond the best farming weapon in this tier. It lets you push against tougher enemies for better experience gains, and it basically tops you up after every battle if you play your cards right. Now, I actually recommend skipping tier 4 weapons entirely depending on how you play, but we'll get into that later. If you decide to play the Temple of Roo section of the game, then definitely get this weapon. It is by far the best weapon in this tier.

Fun fact: The Moonstone Staff used to heal 45 HP on its proc in the game's early days, but it was so busted that they nerfed it down to 30 HP. Even then, it's still one of the best weapons in the game. Let's talk about the passive heal first. 4 HP per turn might not sound like much, but that damage you take during battles adds up. Over a 20 turn battle, you will heal 80 extra HP for free. That's almost a full Greater Healing Potion, and we haven't even talked about the Moonstone Staff's skill procs yet. Spirit of Life randomly heals you for 30 HP, and it procs decently often. When you're sitting at half HP, you'll be surprised how often you get a couple of procs in a row. Anyway, you get the monster healing of Life with the attack power of an endgame weapon, and the weapon fits into any build. I recommend this staff for essentially anyone in the endgame because it's so universally useful.

Summary

So now that you have learned about all the weapons in the game, maybe you're wondering which ones you should be using throughout the game. First, let me say this: every single weapon in the game is good enough to get you through the game area where you receive it. Some are far worse than others, but if want to make a certain weapon work, you can. Secondly, just match your weapon to the skill tree(s) that you are focusing on to maximize your skill + weapon synergy. It's easy to remember and it works. With that said, let me summarize what I think are the best weapons in the game.

Tier 1: These weapons are all carbon copies of each other except that they boost their respective skill tree passive skills where applicable. If I had to choose one, the Blue Wand enables you to proc Ice Weapons for multiple times greater damage than your regular attack at that stage in the game, though that's more about the Ice skill tree than anything. If you want an accelerated start, Ice is the way to go. Otherwise, the White Wand is a very solid pick if you are going Life for healing as you move around the map.

Tier 2: These weapons aren't anything special either... No abilities means that they again only differ by their improvements to your skill procs. At this point in the game, Ice is still extremely strong, by far the best offensive magic in the game.

Tier 3: The Nature Wand is the best weapon of this tier without a doubt, but if you are running Fire or Ice, I would say that the skill + weapon synergy actually takes priority here. At this point of the game, your Ice/Fire Weapons procs are probably still dealing close to 80% of your basic attack damage.

Tier 4: Once again, the Life Staff is the big winner of this weapon tier. It's hard to match the utility of a free 100 HP heal every 20~ turns, available at the beginning of every encounter. No other staff here comes close, though Fire/Ice users may still consider running weapons matching their tree for the extra damage.

Tier 5: The last set of weapons that you get are different from the previous tiers because each one is very powerful in its own way. Again, let me reiterate that I would recommend simply matching your weapon to your most invested skill tree for maximum synergy, but otherwise there are two standout choices here.

The Moonstone Staff is undoubtedly the best staff for random encounters because of its passive healing. From the Mountain Fortress to the end of the game, the number of potions it will save you via its 4 HP heal per turn and random 30 HP bursts is incredible, and the weapon is even more valuable as potentially your only source of sustain if you have not invested in Life skills. There is no equal to this weapon's healing utility when grinding or moving around the map.

In my experience, the best weapon in isolation for boss fights is the Iceheart Staff. The relatively reliable stun utility it provides can disrupt dangerous attack patterns from the enemy, and it oftens saves you from taking multiple turns of damage each cast. The second candidate for this title is unsurprisingly the Moonstone Staff because of its healing utility. You can never have too much HP, and you get the full value of the Moonstone Staff completely via passive regeneration and procs. It's useful even when you are stunned, and there will be times where you survive a stunblast and the remaining HP you have is equal to the healing you received from the staff. It slots well into every single build, and it is just a fantastic default choice if you have any doubts about what to equip.

Boss Analysis

Here, we are going to talk about every single boss in the game. Yes, that includes the Archmagus of Roo even if I recommend skipping him in my walkthrough. Aside from skill tree analysis, this section is probably my favourite and one of the most interesting parts of this guide.

One of the funny things about bosses in Neoquest is that the majority of them can be trivialized by overlevelling. Bosses are generally safe to fight at one level above (with some very important exceptions), and two or three levels above is often overkill. However, you should remember that level difference is tied to hit rate, so being one or two levels down on a boss could be disastrous.

Bosses
Image Name Special Abilities Analysis
Xantan the Foul
  • 20 damage fire/ice blasts

For reference: 20/80 = 25% of your total health at level 12.

Xantan is the very first boss that you will meet in the game, appearing at the end of the fourth floor of the Dank Cave. There is nothing exceptional about him except that he can hit you with 20 damage elemental blasts, dealing a significant chunk of your health at this point in the game. This shouldn't be a problem if you are at the recommended level and have a couple of Standard Healing Potions lying around in case you get very unlucky and he hits you with more elemental blasts than expected.

In summary, keep attacking and keep your HP above 20, maybe 40 if you want to avoid any stress. Clap Xantan and then go get your nice weapon and armour upgrades back at Neopia City.

Kreai
  • 2-turn stun
  • 40 damage fire/ice blasts

For reference: 40/105 = 38% of your total health at level 17.

Kreai is the first boss that you will fight in the Jungle Ruins. Of course, you could do some sequence breaking and fight Gors and Rollay first, but that would make no sense. Anyway, Kreai is what I would consider to be your first real challenge in Neoquest. He hits very hard with his elemental blasts, and his 2-turn stun introduces an element of unpredictability in this fight.

What does this mean for you? It's means that you shouldn't sleep on Kreai's damage output. Any time that you are under 80 HP and not healing is a potential death courtesy of a potent combo: 2-turn stun + elemental blast + elemental blast, wiping out the majority of your health bar without you being able to do anything about it. You would say that the chance is quite low, but it's a long walk back if you lose, and it has certainly happened to me.

As such, the guideline here is to always keep your health above 80, and you should not have any major problems with Kreai.

Gors the Mighty
  • 2-turn stun + 30 damage fire/ice blasts
  • 5-turn poison - 15 damage per tick
  • 20% health restore

This set of skills is much more dangerous than it looks. If Gors dualcasts his stun + elemental blast combo, you will lose with very little counterplay.

For reference:
double stunblast with poison active = 2*(30 + 15)/130 = 69% of your total health at level 22 + unactionable for 3 turns

Gors is the second boss you meet in the Jungle Ruins, and he is a whole different beast than Kreai. Not only is he the hardest part of the Jungle Ruins, but I would go as far as saying that he is one of the toughest bosses in the game. If you didn't read his ability description properly, you might be asking why because he actually deals less damage than Kreai at first glance.

Well, remember when I said that Kreai was dangerous because of his stun followed by heavy elemental damage on the next turn? Gors is the first of the bosses to take this to the next level, hitting you with a 2-turn stun and blasting you with elemental damage in the same turn. This means that not only are you chunked for roughly 23% of your total health at this point of the game in one turn, but you also can't move for two turns afterwards while he wails on you. If that wasn't bad enough, he can also apply a poison DoT on you that doesn't take up his turn after its initial cast, meaning that you can be stunned, poisoned, and getting hit without being able to do anything. Lastly, Gors is the first of several bosses that introduce the nightmare scenario of stunblast chains, chunking your healthbar and leaving you unactionable for a total of 4 turns (or worse). If this happens (and it does happen), you are going back to Neopia City.

Luckily, it's not all doom and gloom versus Gors. You can outlevel him to the point where he is no longer an issue, and you will likely resist some of his poison and stunblast procs. Keep your HP as high as possible without wasting potions in case you get hit with a stunblast, and you should come out on top if you don't get unlucky. Gors is a worthy keeper of the Staff of Ni-tas, required for your next and very significant weapon upgrade.

Rollay Scaleback
  • 3-turn stun
  • 25% HP restore

Honestly, Rollay is a walk in the park compared to Gors. Not only is his level filled with very good opportunities to powerfarm, but he also doesn't possess any outstanding abilities. He's also completely optional if you don't mind fighting Faleinn at full power (don't do this), but his dungeon leads very well into the next section of the game, the Techo Caves.

As I mentioned, Rollay does not have any elemental blasts. His 3-turn stun may look scary, but you will realize that it is harmless without additional threats. I think you could easily survive even 6 turns of being stunned if you aren't underlevelled, and his heal does nothing but prolong the fight a little more, which is no issue if you are running the Nature Wand and haven't burned all your potions.

There is little to say in terms of strategy about Rollay. Don't let your health dip too low and keep attacking until you win. He will give you a very useful item for later.

The Archmagus of Roo
  • 50 damage fire/ice blasts
  • 3-turn stun + 40 damage fire/ice blasts
  • 40% HP restore
  • 50 HP drain

Drain is among the largest HP swings in the game, a whole 100 HP difference in this case.

The Archmagus of Roo is the face of Neoquest and what many would consider to be one of the toughest bosses in the game. I would tend to agree, as he is basically Gors on steroids, and not only does he heal a lot, but he heals more often. On the other hand, he is also what I would call entirely skippable with little consequence because a weapon upgrade is coming at level 42, but you can fight him if you want.

On top of the potential 6-turn stun when the Archmagus dualcasts his stunblast, you'll also notice that he has a lot more going on in the sustain department. One heal will wipe out multiple turns of damage that you dealt, and his drain is arguably even worse, chunking you for 50 damage (roughly 27% of your HP at level 33) and restoring it to himself. As a result, the fight can go much longer than you would expect if you did not level properly.

Like most bosses in this game though, the battle can be made significantly easier just by outlevelling the Archmagus, and even trivialized if you go high enough. If you plan to fight him normally, then just be liberal with your healing, keeping your HP topped up so that you don't get caught off guard when his 3-turn stunblast hits. You will very likely see him go below 100 HP only to see him heal it all back, but again you should win the sustain war with the number of Strong/Greater Healing Potions that you have stashed, and you will soon be able to craft the Life Staff that grants you a free 100 HP heal every battle, so don't skimp on the potion usage.

The Guardians of Elemental Magic
  • 75 HP restore (life)
  • 70 damage ice blast (ice)
  • 70 damage fire blast (fire)
  • Disable - decrease defence (spectral)
  • 4-turn stun (shock)
  • 70 HP drain (all guardians)

The Elemental Magic Guardians are your one stop shop for endgame weapon upgrades. None of them are particularly difficult, but each one has a special ability related to the elemental magic that they represent. There is no particular order that you need to fight the guardians, but I highly recommend that you fight the Guardian of Life Magic first to pick up the Moonstone Staff since it a major QoL upgrade for the rest of the game.

I'd like you to take note right now that every boss in the Mountain Fortress possesses a 70 HP drain, which should not be too surprising or dangerous at this point. Just keep it in mind as I may not touch on it much later on in the section.

The Guardian of Life Magic is what I would call the single most pleasant boss fight in the game. Not only does it not have any elemental magic at all, a rarity for bosses at this stage of the game, but it also drops arguably the best general-purpose weapon in the game as a reward as well.

The only thing really noteworthy thing about the Guardian of Life Magic is that it has considerably higher HP than its other elemental counterparts. It has a decent drain, but given that the only other way for it to attack you is its basic attack, there is little danger in this fight. As long as you keep your HP above 70 and keep wailing away at it, you should have little trouble defeating the Guardian of Life Magic and getting your endgame weapon upgrade.

The next bosses that we will talk about are the Guardian of Ice Magic, and the Guardian of fire Magic. To be entirely honest, these bosses should be a cakewalk after getting your Moonstone Staff. In fact, even the enemies that you fight in their respective areas can deal more damage than each of them can, so they are essentially slightly stronger mooks. The only thing that you need to worry about is getting chunked for 90 damage by the fire guardian or 70 damage by the ice guardian, which is definitely not pleasant, and they also both have a 70 HP drain as well. Since neither of these bosses can stun you, just keep your HP above 100 and you will win guaranteed. As a side note, the Iceheart Staff is incredibly powerful in its own right, not for random encounters but for the endgame bosses, but more on that in the equipment section.

The second last boss in this area is the Guardian of Spectral Magic, and it very well may be the easiest boss in the game at this point. Its only unique skill is a defence disable, which increases the chance of the enemy hitting you on subsequent turns. Given that it can only basic attack you for damage, if you invested in Spectral, this boss may be the easiest one in the game because you will dodge many of its attacks. If not, you will use a few extra potions. Either way, an incredibly easy fight.

The final boss we will talk about in this section is the Guardian of Shock Magic. While a 4-turn stun may sound intimidating, this guy will also do nothing except basic attack you during those turns. Don't forget about the drain though. Despite the lack of elemental damage skills, this guardian may be the most dangerous fight in this section if you get hit a lot with drains or attacks during the stuns, so I recommend that you keep your HP topped up during this encounter. If you are running a Shock-focused build, the Thunderstar Staff is also a very viable endgame weapon choice because it increases the chance of a Shock Weapons proc.

Faleinn
  • 3-turn stun + 70 shock damage
  • 70 damage fire/ice blasts
  • 90 HP drain

Depending on choices that you have made so far in the game, Faleinn can either be the easiest or the absolute most difficult boss fight in the entire game, and I believe that this is by design as a reward for restoring the Kelladrian Medallion to its original form in Sunny City, or as a punishment for neglecting to do so depending on how you look at it. The good news is that if you played the game properly, you can show Faleinn the Kelladrian Medallion in battle to instantly defeat her. However, if you skipped Rollay Scaleback, who drops the Rusty Medallion, or were either too lazy or forgot to meet Gali Yoj at Sunny City to polish it, then you will have to face her head-to-head.

Faleinn is very different from every other boss in the game because she seems to be the only one who almost completely neglects basic attacking in favour of spamming her elemental abilities. It is extremely common to go start this battle and instantly be hit with a stunblast, losing 70 HP (roughly 27% of your total HP at this point) of your health and being unable to move for 3 turns. During those 3 turns, she will very likely follow up with 70 damage elemental blasts as well, or if you are unlucky enough, she will chain another stun and you will lose. In addition to that, she has a 90 HP drain, swinging the fight by an entire 180 HP. This is the only boss outside of the final three where you have to watch your potions because it is very possible to lose most of them just to survive her damage. When you get her low, she will heal, and you will be right back to where you started minus a couple of potions.

Faleinn can one hundred to zero you in just 4 turns, a distinct possibility off any stunblast that you do not resist. Your health has to be 90%+ for the entire duration of the fight, and you may still find yourself back at Neopia City anyway. Do not be surprised at all if this happens.

How this battle usually plays out is that Faleinn will consistently chunk you for 70-90 damage on most turns depending on the ability that she uses. Your opportunity to attack is when you find yourself at near max HP at the beginning of a turn. This could be from outhealing her damage on the previous turn, when you resist an elemental blast, or when she attacks and misses or simply deals low damage. Then you cross your fingers and hope that you do not get stunblasted, and you repeat this cycle until you eventually whittle her down. Of all battles in this game, this is arguably the most RNG-dependent one aside from maybe Jahbal.

As a side note, be sure to restore your Greater Healing Potions from the mobs outside Kal Panning if you fought Faleinn without the medallion. If you forget to do so and pass through the cave to the Two Rings section of the game, you are out of luck. Two Rings enemies do not drop Greater Healing Potions, and the only way to get more is to die and go back to an area where they drop. This is not an option on InSaNe, meaning that you have permanently lost a significant chunk of your health stores for the consecutive endgame boss fights. That being said, if you chose to fight Faleinn without the medallion on InSaNe, you have bigger problems...

Jahbal
  • 3-turn stun + 70 shock damage
  • 90 damage fire/ice blasts
  • 5-turn 50 damage poison
  • Defence disable
  • 150 HP restore
  • 100 HP drain

Jahbal is the first of potentially three big bads that you will meet at the end of the game depending on the difficulty level that you selected, and your encounter with him will set the stage for your other battles. This is never an easy fight, and you will want to make sure that your potion inventory is as close to maxed as possible before coming in, even the Strong and Standard variants.

Much like Faleinn, Jahbal is not only a certified potion drainer, but also a genuine one hundred to zero threat on any given turn thanks to his stunblast. Even worse, his 90 damage elemental blasts will remove 33% of your max HP each turn when they fully land. If you thought that sounded bad, I am not finished yet. He can inflict a 5-turn 50 damage poison on you, and recall that the poison proc is a separate damage tick, meaning that he can also separately chunk you with a stunblast, taking 120 damage from your health bar and still leaving you stunned for 3 turns. If this happens, you will need a miracle to survive both the poison and his attacks at the same time.

Jahbal is no slouch in the sustain department either. Even worse than his elemental damage, his drain siphons 100 HP (37% of your max health, fair and balanced) and adds it to himself. Along with his 150 HP restore, I think you can see why he poses a genuine threat to your potion supply. Do not be surprised if you have spent ten turns landing damage only for him to drain you and then restore himself to full HP while leaving you down a few potions.

Lastly, Jahbal can disable your defence and heavily increase his hit rate against you. While much less dangerous than everything else listed above, consecutive basic attacks still deal heavy damage and force you to heal, so you'll be happy if you decided to invest in Spectral magic.

With all that said, you may ask what the strategy is here. There are a couple of routes that you can take depending on the skill trees that you invested in. The most obvious choice would be the Moonstone Staff because it not only alleviates the heavy potion usage required for Jahbal, but it does so in the form of passive healing from its regeneration and ability, potentially saving your life when you are stun-locked. Regardless of your build, if in doubt, this is the correct choice.

Another interesting choice would be the Thunderstaff Staff if you are running a Shock build. The increased stun chance for Shock Weapons is generally good, the passive stun chance from Staggering Stun can save you from a surprising amount of damage over the course of a fight, and it may also be a lifesaver if it procs when you are stunned. This weapon feels less safe than the Moonstone Staff because you don't get direct healing, but every turn that your enemy is stunned is a turn where you are guaranteed to not take damage. Highly recommended as well.

If you are running an attack-focused Ice build, then the Iceheart Staff may actually optimal choice here because of its boost to the proc rate of your Ice skills, and more importantly, its incredibly powerful active skill, Ice Wind. I will not discuss details about it here because weapons have their own dedicated section, but Ice Wind essentially give you an extra passive stun chance for several turns on top of anything you are inflicting from basic attacks. I have tested this on Evil and it is completely viable.

With the weapon choice out of the way, there is actually quite little to say in terms of specific strategy. Your health must be maxed or close to it every single turn of this fight. If you have been hit by a disable, chances are that you will need to spam Strong Healing Potions until it wears off because his basic attack accuracy will be off the charts. If you are affected by a poison, same idea. Do not take chances gambling that you will resist the poison. A stunblast with poison or disable active all but guarantees a loss even from full health, so you need to minimize the risk here. Do not try to save potions in this fight. Heal every time it is necessary, topping up your HP bar every turn. With some luck and patience, you should win.

Mastermind
  • 120 damage fire/ice blasts
  • 3-turn 50 HP drain

For reference:
120/270 = 44% of your max health.
(120 + 50)/270 = 62.9% of your max health.

The good news is that if you got past Jahbal, Mastermind is a cake walk in the majority of cases. He has no stun at all, so the way to beat him is very straightforward. However, the simplicity of this fight is also what can be a major stress factor depending on how your fight with Jahbal went. If you have a healthy potion supply, then beating Mastermind is a formality. On the other hand, if your supply is starting to dwindle, then you may want to start worrying.

Mastermind dishes out tons of damage with his elemental blasts, each one consumes a full Superior Healing Potion worth of health. He doesn't quite spam them like Faleinn, but if you get unlucky enough, you will be burning potions very quickly. Like other endgame bosses, Mastermind also has a drain as his source of sustain, and it theoretically heals 50% more than the most powerful drains in the game, dealing 150 damage over three turns. This also means that you get three chances to resist the drain rather than one though. However, the important thing to remember about this ability is that it is a passive proc after the initial cast. What this means is that Mastermind can hit you with 120 damage elemental blasts on the same turn that it procs. This is more damage than any single healing potion in the game can recover, and it happens more often than you might think. Because of this, there is a 171 HP threshold that you must meet every turn that Mastermind's drain is active. Otherwise, you risk losing 63% of your HP in one turn and dying. Technically, it's not impossible for him to do multiple times during the drain, so if you really want to be cautious, stay above 191 HP in case he does this combo twice in a row.

With all that said, Mastermind is still a significantly easier fight than Jahbal, and you should have little to no issues if you have invested some points into Shock magic, Fortitude specifically. I will say that I was dangerously close to running out of potions when I ran Evil mode on a fire build because of the small number of points in Fortitude, but it was also just an unlucky run. Full Life and full Shock builds should breeze through him. Just remember, 171 HP whenever a drain is active, or 121 HP otherwise are the minimum values to meet for any given turn.

Xantan Reborn
  • very accurate 50 damage blasts

For being the big bad at the end of the hardest game mode, Xantan Reborn is a walk in the park. He is relatively tanky with 1000 HP to his name, but with no real abilities or even a source of sustain, I am inclined to think that the game devs purposely made him weak because of the two previous bosses that you faced. Unless you are running out of potions of course, which you very well may be at this point.

Xantan's only quirk is that every attack he lands will deal exactly 50 damage, and his hit rate is extremely high. Nearly every turn that he is not stunned, he will hit you for 50 damage. That's less damage than a Strong Healing Potion though. Not only that, but he's also very weak against Spectral magic because of his basic attack dependency, meaning that Spectral users can take full advantage of Evasion and Reflex in this fight.

Regardless of the skill trees that you chose, if you made it this far, then Xantan Reborn should go down without much trouble. You don't have to worry about potion usage anymore, and even your lower tier potions should suffice for healing against him. You are basically fighting a really tanky mook, and it will definitely feel like it.

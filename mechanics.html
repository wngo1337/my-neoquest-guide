Game Mechanics

I may have said it before, but Neoquest isn't the most difficult game in the world. However, the game is no walk in the park if you play it blind. In this section, you will learn about a few key mechanics related to battling. Keep them in mind and you won't lose a single battle the entire game (unless you get really, really unlucky).

Core Game Mechanic: Level Difference + Hit Rate.
Extremely simple, yet perhaps the most important mechanic in the game. Your level compared to the enemy's level determines not only how often you hit/miss, but also how often they hit you. If you find yourself one level under, you start missing a lot and will find your potions diminishing. If you are two levels under or more, it will seem nearly impossible to hit your enemy. Likewise, the same is true for your enemies. Once you gain a level or two over them, it should be smooth sailing. Later on in the skills section, we will talk about how one particular skill tree (Fire) can get around this mechanic, but otherwise treat it as law.

Core Game Mechanic: Elemental Resistance
Elemental attacks are among the most devastating attacks in the game. In the endgame, you would be hard-pressed to find a monster, let alone a boss that doesn't have one of these. Monsters will regularly chunk you for 80-100 damage, and bosses will hit you with a nasty 70 Shock damage + 3 turn stun combo. Your health at max level is 270, by the way. Simply put, it's unreasonable to expect yourself to heal through all this damage. So what's the good news? Unlike basic attacks, elemental damage can be resisted partially or entirely, and you can increase your resistance to it via the Fortitude skill in the Shock skill tree. This means that Fortitude is almost an essential skill if you want to reliably take on the endgame bosses.

You can win a game without Fortitude. You will not have fun doing so. You have been warned.

Minor Game Mechanic: Weapon-Skill Tree Synergy
So here's a fun mechanic. There are a huge number of passive skills in most skill trees that simply activate when you attack. What's so interesting about that? Well, most of them gain increased strength if you use a weapon with a matching elemental type! Not only does the proc rate massively increase in most cases, but the damage of a couple of these skills literally doubles. In the early game, you can consistently be dealing multiple times your regular attack damage when your passive skills proc. Just think about that.

I truly think that these are the only mechanics a player needs to know to win any random encounter, and also most boss fights. You can plan around them accordingly, so there's no excuse for not doing so! Of course, if you want to go for a non-standard build or take some risks, there's nothing wrong with that. Play the game the way that you enjoy it.

Fun fact: you used to be able to heal while stunned by copy-pasting the URL for using a particular healing potion, essentially guaranteeing you victory in every fight. However, I believe that got patched out relatively early on in Neoquest's lifespan, so we're stuck with the chance of getting stunned for 3 rounds and having to replay the entire game on InSaNe.

Hello and welcome to my Neoquest guide. Really, it's more of a discussion than a guide, but call it whatever you like. Neoquest isn't the most popular game right now (it came out in 2004!), but whether it's a newbie or just myself reading this site years down the line, I hope that someone gets something out of it. Ultimately, this guide is a collection of game knowledge accumulated over many years of play and experimentation.

Moving on, there is one important thing that I have to get out of the way: I will not be providing any maps on this site. Sorry if that's what you were searching for, but there's a good reason for that: many others have already done it better than I ever could. If you are in need of them and other detailed game info, feel free to check out neoquest.guide, a very cool resource that has not only a step-by-step guide, but also detailed information on skill proc rates and item drop chances that I imagine would have to have been sourced from the game database itself. You can also use the classic IDNQ if you can find it, but you may have to use Wayback Machine for that one though. However, if you just want to complete the game for the trophies, I can think of a better way to do that...

Meanwhile, we are going to talk about the fun parts, and those are the builds and equipment choices you are going to make throughout the game. Just how broken is Shockwave? Is the Spectral tree worth your time, and is fire really as useless as everyone makes it sound? If these are the kinds of questions you find yourself asking, then you've come to the right place. I think you'll find the results to be far more interesting than what you expect.
